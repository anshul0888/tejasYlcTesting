Beyond Starbucks: discovering the Magnificent Mile as a college student


Emily Bichsel at The Palate at University of Chicago   Posted on 25 April, 2016

At first glance, the Loop and the Magnificent Mile Chicago seem decidedly corporate. Flagship stores of international brands stand next to Starbucks and other commercial coffee chains. As a tourist or business person, finding a unique spot to grab a snack after an exhausting afternoon of shopping or meetings can seem daunting. But if you know where to look, downtown Chicago offers hidden gems with excellent food and atmosphere, right off the MagMile and Loop. The following list is oriented north to south along Michigan Avenue.

1) Hendrickx Belgian Bread Crafter, Chicago

On the northern side of the Shops at 900 (a very nice mall home to Bloomingdale’s, J.Crew, and Maxx Mara, among others) sits this unassuming church of French pastries. When I first went, I questioned whether I was in the right place, as there was little signage, but trust Google Maps and go in. Hendrickx patiserrie serves up traditional French pastries in a quaint setting. You can’t go wrong whatever you order, but the patissiers at Hendrickx make a mean french croissant. I almost always head straight for the carbs, but they also have a lunch menu of salads, soups, and sandwiches.

2) 40 Carrots

Hidden way in the back of the Children’s department on the 6th floor of Bloomingdale’s, lies this Pantheon of frozen yogurt ice cream and healthy bites. The Chicago outpost of the classic New York restaurant serves up the same amazing yogurt in a less hectic setting than the New York original. 40 Carrots is especially good for those seeking healthy options; their menu contains bright, creative and delicious salads and wraps that even my dad (a devoted meat-and-potatoes Midwesterner) endorses.

3) Amorino Gelato Ice Cream

Within Watertower Place (another large mall) you can find this novel Paris gelato shop. They serve their gelato in the shape of a rose and have many flavors to choose from. There are plenty of benches and tables in the mall to sit down and relax, or keep that shopping game strong and explore one of the mall’s eight floors of conspicuous consumerism.

4) Eataly

Ok, so Eataly isn’t exactly a secret, but I think people tend to overlook all of the bounty it has to offer. For first timers, the bustling food emporium can seem overwhelming, and a lack of available seating may be a turn off for those looking for a respite from the crowds of Michigan Avenue. The Gelato, Pastry, Nutella, Panini, and Lavazza coffee bars are all great, but they tend to be loud and crowded. Look beyond the first floor and you shall be rewarded. Upstairs there is an antipasti restaurant called Piazza which serves wine, cheese boards, salads, cured meats and other typical Italian “starters.” You can sit at a counter and watch chefs prepare your fresh cheeses and slice cured meats for lunch or an afternoon snack in a casual setting. I love having a relaxed lunch with friends and ordering several dishes to share.

If you’re just looking for a snack or a quick lunch, the focaccia bar is the place to be. Hands down, Eataly focaccia bar is my favorite place on Michigan Avenue – it is a college student’s best friend. For the nominal price of around $3.60 you can have a huge slice of freshly-made, artisanal focaccia bread that is sure to satisfy even the pickiest of gastronomes. Toppings (and prices) vary, but I always stick to the classic margherita. You can also pick up a baguette or a giant chocolate chip cookie for the road.

5) Shake Shack, Chicago

Yes, everyone has heard of Shake Shack and its burgers and it isn’t a Chicago original, but for those of us with a sweet tooth, each Chicago location (one across from Eataly on East Ohio and another in the Chicago Athletic Association Hotel across from the Art Institute) has something magical to offer. Shake Shack has custom concretes that blend in some of Chicago’s finest confections. The River North location offers a killer combination of Bang Bang Pie s’mores pie blended into vanilla and chocolate custard. Why choose one dessert when you can have it all? Other offerings include concretes with truffle cookie dough and Glazed and Infused doughnuts. The local options at each Shake Shack location are different so choose wisely…

6) West Town Bakery

Travel about a block or two further west on East Ohio (away from Michigan Avenue) and you will find West Town Bakery and Diner. They offer fun sweets (think multicolored cake balls) and sandwiches in a small space. My favorite sweet is the trail mix scone with its buttermilk flavor and nutty texture. They also offer (equally amazing) trail mix cookies, cinnamon buns, danishes, croissants, doughnuts, and muffins.

7) Doughnut Vault or Firecakes Donuts

Located just a few blocks west off Michigan on Franklin Street, the Doughnut Vault serves hot, fresh donuts and artisanal coffee until supplies run out. Lines are often long, and they will sell out, especially on weekends so get there early and be aware that disappointment is a possibility. But the risk is worth the reward for true doughnut lovers. For those left disappointment, you can walk back towards Michigan Avenue and take a detour to Firecakes, where they serve equally sinful doughnut confections and espresso. Bonus: Firecakes is open until midnight Thursday-Saturday (10:30 PM all other days) so all of you party-animals can grab some fuel before heading out to clubs and bars! Firecakes is also good for post-dinner dessert, or just whenever, because you can’t ask why about doughnuts…

8) Stan’s Donuts and/or LYFE Kitchen

Tucked away beneath Northwestern Medical Center, the California doughnut shop, Stans Donuts, Stan’s Donuts, serves large fluffy donuts sure to please those of us who need a little carb love in their life. For the health conscious LYFE Kitchen offers up healthy eats in a bright setting just within the medical center – a stone’s throw from Stan’s. I know, eating in a medical center sounds terrible, but it’s really like a standalone restaurant that happens to be connected to the medical center. LYFE kitchen is a chain of sorts, but it doesn’t feel corporate and serves reasonably creative and delicious fresh food.M

9) Toni Patisserie and Cafe

Toni Patisserie is conveniently located across from Millennium Park. They dish out traditional pastries, espresso, quiches, breakfast items and salads in a quaint la café setting. This patisserie is family owned and is an excellent option for lunch as well as dessert.

10) Cafecito Cuban Coffee

If you’re looking for an inexpensive lunch near the Art Institute and want to stay away from Dunkin Donuts and Subway, head a few blocks south to Cafecito. Located in a hostel, this Café Cubano has a casual vibe and serves up hot Cuban sandwiches, coffee, and sides to hungry students, wanderers, and business people on lunch break. Craving something sweet (…I always am)? Then order one of the flavored milkshake/smoothies to go with your meal.

As you can see, if you travel a few steps off the beaten path, Downtown Chicago becomes a veritable playground of culinary options (and this list only encompasses café fare!). So go forth and explore like a local - because life has more to offer than corporate coffee chains.
