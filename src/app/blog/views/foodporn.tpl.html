SEVEN #foodporn worthy eats in boston


  Posted on 31 July, 2015

Boston is one of the most beautiful cities in the US and what’s better is that it makes for the perfect weekend getaway. It’s a cultural powerhouse, chockfull of American history but what you might not know is that Boston is home to an extremely diverse culinary scene that will leave you craving dishes – even on the ride back home. I’m a native New Yorker and even though I’m surrounded by some of the best food on the planet, I still dream of the following seven foods I ate in Boston.

1) Lobster Tail - Maria’s Pastry Shop, North End

One of the most contested culinary debates in Boston is “who makes the best cannoli?” There are two rivals, Mike’s Pastry and Maria’s Pastry Shop. The wait to get into either in the Italian part of town, North End, can exceed an hour even on a lull weekday afternoon. While I’m not picking sides what I will say is that the more crave-worthy pastry is actually Maria’s ‘Lobster Tail’. This beast weighing (what feels like) ½ a pound is crispy and buttery with a very flakey outside (watch out because when you crunch into it, it will get everywhere!), and a very rich and sweet ricotta cream hidden inside. Just look at how it’s gargantuan size compares to the little cannoli! Poor little cannoli! This is an awesome pastry and definitely #foodporn worthy.

2) Szechuan Spicy Tofu - Gourmet Dumpling House

In a sea of amazing Italian restaurants I was craving authentic Chinese food one day. My cousin recommended Gourmet Dumpling House and I was very impressed! I’ve spent a lot of time in New York’s Chinatown and Flushing so I know a thing or two about what makes for a great dish and boy did the Szechuan Spicy Tofu deliver! This dish is actually the Szechuan Spicy Sliced Fish that is featured on the Food Network’s Best Thing I Ever Ate but we subbed in tofu since I was dining with vegetarians. Spicy is not the word to describe it – it’s mouth numbing and induces a runny nose on impact but we kept going back for spoonfuls until the bowl was licked clean! Soft chunks of fresh tofu make a great vehicle for the pungent peppercorns and red chilies that take over this dish. Again, #foodporn worthy and worth the trip!

3) Lobster Fra Diavolo - The Daily Catch, North End

This is hands down, one of the best Italian restaurants I have ever been to! It can barely seat 10 people and there is often a wait (they have other locations but this is the original) and the aroma from the kitchen is absolutely mouthwatering. Their signature dish is the Lobster Fra Diavolo, which in Italian translates to “prepared with tomato sauce and usually seasoned with garlic, oregano and red hot pepper”. This dish is loaded with littlenecks, mussels, and shrimp but the star of the show is the 1 ¼ lb. lobster who just screams “eat me!” Try the other dishes including the Aglio Olio, and the squid ink pasta with monkfish and mushrooms. Can we say major #foodporn

4) Lobster Ravioli – Giacomo’s Restorante, North End

You know how in many Italian restaurants when you order lobster ravioli you end up with miniscule little pieces of Lobster hidden in other junk? Well that doesn’t happen here! There is an hour-long wait at Giacomo’s Restorante for a reason – everything is fabulous, everything is super fresh and when you order lobster ravioli – you get LOBSTER ravioli. The cream sauce is not overpowering and you can actually taste lobster! Again #foodporn

5) Any bread made at Bricco Panetteria, North End

I almost don’t want to give away my secret that is Bricco Panetteria! Bricco has several restaurants under its name and its bread comes from a hidden gem tucked away in Boston’s North End. Fortunately it’s open to the public and fortunately I wandered down a small, discrete alleyway to encounter this bread heaven! The first time I came I brought a hot, crusty baguette and polished the whole thing off with my cousins in 5 minutes! The next day I came back and did the same thing with the olive loaf. My goal in life is to try all their bread and I will take another trip to Boston just for this! #foodporn #getinmybelly.

6) Egg in a Jar - West Bridge, Kendall Square

This is one of the coolest (duck) egg dishes I’ve eaten since this creamy, eggy, custard-like umami explosion is neatly packaged in a mini mason jar at West Bridge. It comes topped with hen of the woods mushrooms, artichokes and “crispy skin”. It’s like a jar of the best stuff on earth and is definitely something I crave! #foodpornBoston.

7) Any ice cream and gelato from Toscanini’s Ice Cream, Cambridge

Toscanini’s Ice cream is absolutely awesome and has some wicked delicious flavors. The mango sorbet is to die for – not too sweet with a little tang and really bright notes. I’m also in love with Salted Saffron, Vienna Finger Cookie and Cocoa Pudding. What’s not to love about this ultimate #foodporn
