Suitcase challenge: I Tried Packing 130 Items Into a Carry On Suitcase Just Like Rachel Grant . . .

By Aarti Kanodia   Posted on 11 August, 2016

When Rachel Grant (Bond Girl and Angelina Jolie’s stunt double!) told me that she is making a video where she packs 130 items into a small carry on bag I thought she was nuts! I pack (what I think is) 130 items into 2 large suitcases that I check in when traveling overseas for three weeks at a time. I can’t even fit enough clothes and shoes into a carry on for an OVERNIGHT stay! But Rachel Grant makes it look effortless in the video she just made below so I decided to challenge myself and try packing at least 130 items into a carry-on suitcase using her tips (the photographic evidence is below)!

In the video Rachel demonstrates that she can pack 130 items, including 23 items of clothing (not including under clothes) and 5 pairs of shoes. She packs them all into a Biaggi “zipsak”, a soft piece of foldable carry-on luggage from a New York based company known for its innovative design. Her tips and tricks include:

  • Make good use of rubber bands, Ziploc bags and “packing cubes” to compress clothes and compartmentalize items (Biaggi also has “Zipcubes”)

  • Roll clothes to save space

  • Stuff shoes with sock balls – this will also help your shoes keep their shape

  • Rubber band shoes together to save space

  • Fold similar shaped clothes together

  • Pick lightweight fabrics (like silk, cotton etc.)

  • Place heavier items at the bottom of the bag

    • The Challenge

    After watching Rachel I was inspired to try this and I knew it would be a challenge for a “high maintenance packer” like myself. I definitely thought that the only way I could fit 130 items into a carry on was if the following happened:

  • 120 items somehow made their way into the below toilet bag and that included makeup, hair ties, my snore strips (yes I snore!), and if every hairclip and Band-Aid counted

  • I only had to pack underwear and toe socks into the suitcase

  • I could fill the suitcase with M&Ms and call it a day

    • But I decided to give it a shot! I figured if Rachel can do it, as an avid world traveler, then I could too. I take at least 5-6 vacations per year and could certainly use the packing help.

    What I Used to Try and Fit 130 Items into My Bag

    The Carry-on Bag: On occasion I use the below carry-on bag. It’s a Tommy Hilfiger canvas bag so it’s somewhat pliable but not as pliable as the Biaggi Zipsak that Rachel used. It also doesn’t have as many pockets but it does have a large front pocket and a plastic waterproof pouch on the inside for additional storage opportunities.

    Ziploc Bags and rubber bands: The Ziploc bags are key to fitting clothing into the carry on since it really helps compress the fabrics and you can let all the air out (I’m certain you can fit even more clothing by using the vacuum bags that really get the air out). The rubber bands came in handy to keep the shoes together and were also very helpful. I didn’t have “travel cubes” to fit the bags into so I will certainly be purchasing them before my next trip.

    The Results

    I was up to the challenge of getting at least 130 items into my carry on suitcase and pretended I was going on a beach vacation to Mexico. I picked mostly lightweight clothing from silk tops to cotton capris but did throw in some more practical items even though they are bulky – like a pair of jeans. Overall my packing list included:

    • - 23 pieces of clothing (just like Rachel!) which included pants, shorts, tops, a pair of jeans and a swimsuit
    • - 6 pieces of gym wear including tops/sports bras/workout pants
    • - 8 pairs of underwear
    • - 8 bras
    • - 5 pairs of shoes
    • - 1 pair of sunglasses + 1 pair reading glasses
    • - 3 belts
    • - 10 socks (toe socks for working out)
    • - hair straightener
    • - phone charger/computer charger
    • - a small notebook
    • - 63 items in a makeup bag AND a toilet bag which included eye masks, sunblock, medication, toothpaste, shampoo, conditioner etc.

    TOTAL = 132 items!

    Clothes and shoes!
    Gym clothes + miscellaneous
    What worked:

  • Rolling up all the clothing that is made of light material was key! I managed to roll up (almost) everything and even take most of the air out of the bags.

  • Rubber banding shoes together kept them in place!

  • Stuffing socks into shoes reduced space and the shoes kept their shape

    • What did not work:

  • Anything bulky took up too much space, including jeans! I decided to wear one pair of jeans to the airport and swapped it out for another pair of cotton capris. Jeans also didn’t fit into Ziploc bags very well

  • High-heel shoes didn’t really fit and were an odd shape so those got swapped out for dressy flats

  • Bulky bras also needed to be swapped out for “bralets” or tank tops with built-in-bras

    • Challenge Successful!

    Despite having little self-confidence upon hearing the words “130 items in a carry on suitcase”, using Rachel’s tips I managed to pack just over 130 items (it took a while)! I definitely think that having the packing cubes will help and getting a more pliable carry on bag like the ones Biaggi sells could make the process more efficient.

    Our challenge to Rachel: how many items can you fit in a carry-on bag for a ski trip? Just kidding!
