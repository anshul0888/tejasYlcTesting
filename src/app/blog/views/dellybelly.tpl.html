Indian Street Food That wont give you a Delhi-Belly


Aarti Kanodia & Kunal Jain   Posted on 17 May, 2015

Let’s face it, the best food in India is made on the streets by someone who has been making the same thing day in and day out for decades. Unfortunately most of these establishments cause "Delhi Belly" - major stomach problems that can last days and ruin a vacation. There are, however several street stalls that have proven the test of time and that we would consider safe for the traveler with a sensitive stomach. Many of the foods from these stalls are fully cooked and/or made with filtered water (that’s just the rumor on the street).

A note about who we are:

Kunal may have been born in Agra but has spent over a decade abroad in Germany, New York, Dubai and Singapore. Despite being Indian by blood his exposure to the foreign world has rendered his stomach “sensitive” and he has little tolerance for Indian street food (he can barely eat American fast food without spending hours on the toilet).

Aarti may be Indian by descent but she is a true “ABCD” born and raised in New York. Her stomach is so sensitive when she travels to India that when she was younger she would brush her teeth with filtered Bisleri water – to the amusement of her family

New Delhi, M-block Market in Greater Kailash street stall: Momo-wala

You won’t be able to resist the fresh Momos from the street side shops as you make your way onto Greater Kailash Part 1 – M block market or GK1 M block as its popularly known to all the Delhites. This momo place has been around for a very long time and was always a place checked out by Kunal when he visited Delhi. The momos come with an intense red chilli sauce and to the surprise of Kunal it never made him make a dash for the neighboring Mcdonald's rest room!

Agra, Uttar Pradesh Street stall: Dauji - Sri Dauji Mishthan Bhandar

Dauji is a popular street stall in Agra specializing in rabri (a milk-based dessert available only in the summers), gajar ka halwa (a carrot dessert made only in the winter), samosas (fried triangles usually stuffed with potato) and jelebis (fried rings of crispy dough soaked in orange sugar syrup). This is one of Kunal’s favorite places and he hasn’t gotten any stomach issues here!

Bareilly,Uttar Pradesh Street stall: Deenanath Lassi Wala

Bareilly is home to this famous “Lassi Wala” – a man who specializes in the art of making sweet and savory yogurt-based drinks for generations. Kunal always made it a point to force his parents to stop for lassi on their way to Agra from Nainital every time despite the chaotic traffic in the city. This tangy, refreshing beverage comes in flavors like mango, saffron and pistachio and is worth the trouble!

New Delhi, South Extension Part 1 Street stall: “Bhel Puri”

Before Haldiram’s took over Delhi, everyone would agree that the bhel puri in South Extension (South Ex) Part 1 was the place to visit if you were craving some. The bhel puri was so good that people didn’t mind eating here even in the summer and again Kunal never got Delhi belly from eating a plate or two of this delicious street-side food. The bhel puri was so good that there were long and often heated discussions in his family whether South Ex’s bhel puri was better than Mumbai’s street side bhel puri!

Chandni Chowk, New Delhi Street stall: Parathe Wali Gali (the road is full of people making stuffed breads)

Parathe Wali Gali is an experience and the location alone is worth a trip. This tiny street is tucked away in “Old Delhi” otherwise known as Chandni Chowk and is surrounded by narrow winding streets that specialize in different foods and housewares. However some would argue that Parathe Wali Gali is the best street since it is full of vendors serving hot, freshly made parathas, Indian style breads stuffed with everything from cauliflower to peas to radish. We love it and never got sick! Pick a stall and fill your tummy!

Nainital (neat Tibetian Market), Uttaranchal Street Stall:Sonam Fast Food

Kunal frequented Sonam during his days in boarding school. Not only were the momos and other Tibetian delicacies affordable but Sonam was also a great place to meet girls! Despite a number of new Tibetan stalls and restaurants that have opened up in Nainital, this one remains to be a hit with the locals - and doesn’t have us running for the bathroom!

Kolkata, West Bengal Street Stall: “The pani puri guy on Russell Street”

This is my favorite pani puri on the planet – hands down (don’t tell my aunt that!). The best part is I haven’t gotten sick from this stall and I’ve eaten here more than seven times between 2009 and 2015. The pani puris are very fresh and the water is sublime – not too spicy but very tangy and flavorful. You might want to turn your head as workers dunk their entire hands in the metal pails of this minty, spicy water but you can’t beat this experience ANYWHERE.

Kolkata, West Bengal Street Stall: “Share-bazaar ki chai” (Chai by the Stock Exchange)

Sunday mornings (and any other mornings) are great for sipping this amazing chai by Kolkata’s Stock Exchange district. Drive down this narrow lane past “The rich kids of Kolkata” driving all the luxury foreign cars and head over to this stall. You don’t need to locate it since one of the “chai-wallas” will find you and ask for your order. Order the chai – there is only one type – its milky, frothy and flavored with rich cardamom and saffron. It also has the perfect amount of sweetness, which many chai-wallas can’t get right. Also get the famous toast, which is thick, buttery and melts in your mouth. This is the most perfect breakfast or snack that anyone could ask for – and perfectly fine on anyone’s sensitive stomach.
