Location: Pitampura

this 946CP Machoke refused to come quietly with a Great Ball and a Great Throw; it’s a good thing I had Ultra Balls. A Great Ball is a 1.5x multiplier and an Ultra Ball is a 2x multiplier, so an Ultra Ball has a higher chance of capture.

Pokémon GO Tip #1

Always be prepped to capture high CP Pokémon, by stocking up at least 50 Pokéballs (or 10 Great Balls and 5 Ultra Balls) and 10 Razz Berries, at all times. Unless you want to risk catching that Pikachu…

Pokémon GO Tip #2

Even then, you may want to start curving your throw, to add that extra kick to the capture. Pokémon like Abra have a flee rate of 99%, so your odds if you miss the first ball are 0.5%. Good luck with that.

Where in Delhi do you have the highest chances to catch Pokémon?

1. Lodhi Gardens: Over 45 Pokéstops

Popularly known as a Slowpoke and Ponyta nest. The place is so huge, you can easily find Growlithe, Sandshrew, Mankey, Machop, Cubone, Geodude, Doduo, Diglett, Rhyhorn and Pokémon like Kabuto, Squirtle, Psyduck, and Goldeen near the strip of water. Their evolved forms spawn for players with higher levels. You can find Charmander there too depending upon the time of day and your luck. As you can see in the sightings, that Jolteon gave me the slip. This place closes around 9 though, for stubborn PokémonGo trainers.

2. Haus Khas Village Lake and Deer Park: Over 30 Pokéstops

Delhi’s Water Pokémon nest and my personal favorite spot to go hunting. The lures are plenty, and you can hunt past midnight. The area between the lake and HKV has the most spawn rate, and you can catch Staryu, Magikarp, Horsea, Goldeen, Krabby, Kabuto, Poliwag, Psyduck and Omanyte in abundance. You can commonly find Geodude, Doduo, Mankey, Machop, Cubone, Rhyhorn and uncommonly stumble upon Exeggcute, Hitmonlee and Squirtle.

Pokémon GO Tip #3

Join a local community or group on FB for updates on nest locations rare Pokémon sightings and game features.

3. Qutub Minar: 14 Pokéstops

Location: Qutub Minar

The close proximity of the Pokestops makes this a fun place to catch Pokémon like Cubone, Mankey, Sandshrew, Rhyhorn, Geodude and Charmander. The place is also the haunt for Drowzee, Charmeleon and Magmar.

4. Netaji Subhash Place: 11 Pokéstops

This place has far-off Pokéstops, but its open 24/7 and has a high spawn rate for Domestic Pokémon. They’re the obvious names I haven’t been taking all this while namely, Pidgey, Ratata, Meowth, Ekans, Spearow and Zubat. However, you do find Machop, Sandshrew, Geodude, Growlithe, Doduo and Seel, but not as much as the others. This one time, after a long hunt, while returning feeling really drowsy, I caught a … wait for it …

As you can see, this fat little guy made me run around to catch him. The Pokémon Go GPS can be a real pain sometimes, pay close attention to your sightings if you want to catch that unregistered Pokémon. Even though he was just a 14CP Pokémon, I fed him a berry, and used a Great Ball; totally unnecessary, but I couldn’t risk it *blush. Speaking of which, for those of you finding New Pokémon…

Pokémon GO Tip #4

Now that the ‘Nearby’ Pokémon Go GPS has been replaced with ’Sightings’, most Pokémon spawn for about ten minutes and no longer appear in any sort of order. Any Pokémon on the list could be any relative distance from your location. So you better hustle …

5. Select Citywalk, Saket : 6 Pokéstops

Two reasons why you should hunt here, one, it’s air-conditioned, unlike every other location, you get to catch Pokémon without sweating, and two, it’s a fun place for a Pokédate with BAE *blush

Growlithe, Sandshrew, Exeggcute, Mankey, Geodude, Rhyhorn, Doduo, Ponyta and other Domestic Pokémon are common here. I guess I was lucky to find Charmander here too.

Pokémon GO Hack For Commuters

Another fun way to catch Pokémon is, riding the Delhi Metro.

Location: Kashmere Gate Metro Station

Almost every station has at least 1 Pokéstop, and that’s quite a blessing if you’re taking it to work in the morning. You know you’ll be well supplied by the time you reach office. Now all you have to do is catch Pokémon on the sly without your boss catching you.

And if you’re lucky, you can catch Pokémon without moving a muscle, except your finger muscles duh! Gotta curve those Pokéballs…

Location: Pitampura

These are the kind of Pokémon I wake up to sometimes; free Pokémon Go Candy honestly. You need to catch, hatch, transfer and evolve plenty of Pokémon to level up. Which is why, sometimes I like to practice getting an Excellent Throw when I’m home. If the Pokémon is really close to you, it’s worth a shot. Otherwise, always throw Curveballs.

Location: Pitampura

Pokémon GO Hack For Max XP: The Lucky Egg Evolution Trick

Once you have close to 60-70 Pokémon, ready to evolve, open a Lucky Egg and keep evolving your Pokémon for the 30min. that the egg lasts. You’ll get 1000XP for every evolution instead of 500XP, which you would earn normally. Evolving 60 Pokémon with a Lucky Egg can earn you 60,000+XP, which can boost you from Level 8 to Level 10 and similarly, Level 10 to Level 11.

Finally, the part, everyone’s been waiting for- Where to catch Pikachu
So, I would say you have a better chance trying to hatch Pikachu, since you get them from 2km eggs, which is not so bad, but you also can get 14 other Pokémon, of which some might make you cry. However, you can try the Burari Bypass, as some players claim it’s a nest. Shhh….

This leads us to our final tip …

Pokémon GO Tip #5

Never forget to add an Egg to the Incubator once one hatches. There is nothing worse, than walking 10km, without a single egg hatching.

Now Go Catch’em All!!!
