An Amazing Morning in Brooklyn with the Fabulous Vicki Winters


Aarti Kanodia   Posted on 23 March, 2016

ACME Smoked Fish
30 Gem Street
Greenpoint, Brooklyn

Over the past several months I’ve been attending events hosted by Travel Massive, which creates opportunities to meet other like-minded people who are passionate about travel and exploring the world. During one such event In Manhattan I met Vicki Winters, a petite fireball who packs a punch of energy – and who I noticed right away because of her sequined vintage Versace hat. Vicki is a professional blogger whose brands My Big Fat Mouth and the Vicki Winter’s Show, is contagiously entertaining and she showcases her local knowledge for all things NYC and has a fresh perspective on world travel. When she rattled off a laundry list of her favorite things to do in the city, at the top of her list is visit to ACME Smoked Fish – which I had never heard of and being a smoked salmon lover I was intrigued. It turns out that on Friday mornings ACME Smoked Fish allows the public to purchase some of the best smoked fish which they usually only sell wholesale to fancy places like Wholefoods, Dean and Deluca, and even the legendary Zabars. I was determined to make a visit and was super over-the-moon excited when Vicki finally had a Friday morning free!

The instructions I received from Vicki were “meet me at 30 Gem Street in Greenpoint, Brooklyn – it’s an industrial part of town and there are virtually no signs or marked doors and it’s cash only”. I literally had no idea what to expect but made my way to the boarder of Williamsburg and Greenpoint, down a deserted street and knew I was in the right place when I saw forklifts carrying pallets of ACME boxes. As I walked closer I couldn’t find any signs for ACME Fish – only ones that said to “beware of rat poison”. But then all of a sudden an unmarked gray door opened with people carrying large white bags. Every few minutes people would enter and leave from this unmarked door and it made the experience all the more intriguing.

Vicki showed up on her bicycle (she enjoys biking across the Williamsburg Bridge from Tribeca) and we walked into ACME’s giant warehouse. The crowd was definitely local – older men chatting about how they have been coming for years, young co-workers looking for an excellent lunch and a Jewish couple debating how much lox and sturgeon to buy for Sunday brunch. We waited 45 minutes on a long line that snaked into a warehouse that was a feast for the eyes! Sitting on tables were their famous packages of sturgeon, lox, gravlax and various whitefish spreads all at wholesale prices!).

But the real jewel is their lox “counter” where you can sample ANYTHING! There was lemon-pepper lox, gravlax, pastrami lox, and the famous “David Burke Special” – which was fantastic! There was another special – salmon poke – thick chunks of the best smoked salmon, avocado, shallots, ginger and lemon – insanely delicious! Did I mention the prices are wholesale as well and that I ended up getting twice the amount of lox than I was expecting!?!

If you are visiting New York and really want to live like a local then ACME Smoked Fish should definitely be on your list. If you live in NYC and are looking to experience the best smoked fish the city has to offer then this hidden gem in Brooklyn is the place to be on Friday mornings. A big Thank You to Vicki for helping me find my smoked salmon nirvana! But wait . . . our food tour wasn’t over!

The Bagel Store
349 Bedford
Avenue Brooklyn, NY 11211

I didn’t think that anything could beat the ACME Smoked Fish experience but the icing on the cake was definitely a trip to The Bagel Store. This was a special trip for two reasons: A) I could start eating my lox right away on one of their fabulous bagels and B) The Bagel Store is home of the (now) world famous rainbow bagel. I like to think that Vicki discovered this now sacred rainbow bagel – because when we went there it wasn’t world famous just yet, rather it became an internet sensation just a few short weeks later. Did I try it ?– not really – but I did find it beautiful and Vicki purchased several for a party she was hosting. They are not cheap according to bagel standards – at $3.95 – but they make beautiful for pictures.

Best of all you can order them with Birthday Cake (Funfetti) Cream Cheese as well as other colors (“Rainbow“Cake Cream Cheese).
