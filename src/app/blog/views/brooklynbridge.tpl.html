Beyond Times Square & The Brooklyn Bridge

Margarita Irene Zulueta   Posted on 5 July, 2015

Anyone headed to New York is bombarded with information about what to do, when to do it, where to do it and why. With so much information, it is difficult to figure out what exactly you want to do after you finish looking at the main attractions.

We, at Your Local Cousin, want you to have a more authentic NYC experience. If you are a first time visitor, we definitely want you to check out the attractions that have made New York an internationally famous location. But if you want to explore a little more then we’ve got you covered too.

Here are samplings of some of our favorite local experiences nearby Times Square & the Brooklyn Bridge:

Manhattan:

Okay so you’ve hit up Times Square, saw a couple of Broadway shows, snapped some pictures of Rockefeller Center, and pretty much feel like you’ve conquered Midtown. What do you do next?

MUST TRY IN MANHATTAN:

* The quick eats: Midtown is known as the tourist area, but it is also the area where many NYC residents go to work, which means that there are a ton of delicious but fast places to grab a meal. In the summer Madison Square Park Eats features a ton of small local restaurants showing off their delicious food. Just head down Times Square in between 40th street and 42nd street and sample some of the yummy foods.

* Bryant Park:Yes this is a major tourist attraction because it is a gorgeous park and it is located next to the famous New York Public Library building, but Bryant Park features a lot of activities that are a great way to while the time away. Head to the games corner for a game of chess, try your hand at Ping Pong or get a waffle from the Wafels & Dinges stand. No matter what it’ll definitely be a good time. Additionally, for all Game of Thrones fans, the HBO store is located on 43rd and 6th avenue and has got a ton of GoT swag available for purchase.

Brooklyn:


So you go to Brooklyn, have a fun stroll across the Brooklyn Bridge, ride the carousel in the park, and grab some ice cream at the Brooklyn Ice Cream Factory. But now you want to do a little something more. We’ve got some options for you!

MUST TRY IN THE DUMBO AREA:

* The POWERHOUSE Arena: A great bookstore that features a wide selection of art books and Brooklyn pride products. They also sometimes host art exhibits that are perfect for getting a taste of the Brooklyn art

* One Girl Cookies: a dessert bakery located near the Brooklyn Bridge Park that is actually, yes well known for their cookies, but also famous for their whoopee pies. The pies aren’t very big but man they are tasty. They’re great to munch on when strolling around the DUMBO area.

* Go Gallery Hopping: Okay, this is not an establishment, but it is a fun activity to do in DUMBO when you’ve got the time. The area under the Brooklyn Bridge holds a yearly arts festival called the DUMBO Arts Fest every fall, so if you’re there during that time go check it out. If not then hey you’ve still got some amazing galleries in the DUMBO area that you can go and take a look at.

If you want to try a different neighborhood:


Go to Fort Greene! Just a five to ten minute walk from Barclays Center and around a 20-minute walk from the Brooklyn Bridge, this neighborhood has some well-known but relatively offbeat establishments to interest all sorts of travelers.

WHAT'S UNIQUE IN NYC:

* Habana Outpost: On days when the weather is great and even when it’s not, this Cuban restaurant has some delicious eats that you and your party can enjoy in their outdoor picnic table area. Take in the Fort Greene atmosphere with a delicious frozen margarita and some fantastic grilled corn all ready to make your mouth water.

* UrbanGlass: A glassblowing studio in Fort Greene, that is located right next to the Brooklyn Academy of Music (more on that later), UrbanGlass is an awesome spot to grab some coffee and treats and stare at the amazing glasswork on display. The exhibit is located on the ground floor of the building, which also houses the workspaces on higher floors. For all those that are interested, you can check their calendar to sign up for weekend workshops and test out your glassblowing skills!

* Brooklyn Academy of Music: Well-known amongst the locals, the BAM as it is affectionately called, hosts a variety of events that appeal to anyone who loves the arts. From film talks to concerts to dance performances, the BAM’s awesome calendar is a great source to find cool local events.​
