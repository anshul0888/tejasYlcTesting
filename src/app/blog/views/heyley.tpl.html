Lecturer, PhD, Cabin Crew, Blogger and a Traveler – Meet Hayley


Hayley Stainton   Posted on 2 July, 2016

Kunal: What got you into traveling and how long have you been doing this?

Hayley: I always wanted to travel since I was a child. I chose the GCSE options that I thought would give me the best chances (I chose Italian, Spanish, French, IT and drama) and then went on to pursue travel and tourism courses at college and university. My first trip without my parents was at age 18 and I've never looked back!

Kunal: How many countries have you been to so far, what places are you visiting in the year ahead?

Hayley: According to my Trip Advisor cities I've visited app I have travelled to 197 cities and 41 countries which equates to 26% of the world apparently! This year so far I have been to the Galapagos Islands, Amazon rainforest, Mexico and Miami. I also have a trip booked to Greece where my husband and I will travel north-south and a trip booked for Christmas in Abu Dhabi. I am also hoping to book a little weekend away for my 30th birthday in November!

Kunal: Are you the kind of traveler that likes to plan to the very last detail? If yes, what resources for travel planning do you use?

Hayley: No not really, I always buy a Lonely Planet for my trip (I have a pretty cool collection now) and I use Trip Advisor a lot to find the best places to go. Other than I generally go with the flow. For accommodation I tend to use Airbnb and for flights I tend to use Skyscanner.

Kunal: What factors do you consider when planning what places you want to visit?

Hayley: At the moment I travel just me and my husband so I don't really consider much to be honest apart from price (we are in the process of buying a new house so we don't want to spend too much just now). But when we do hear the tapping of tiny little feet I will be a lot more cautious to research the safety aspects of destinations and to make sure that the accommodation we choose is suitable for children.

Kunal: Would love to hear some of your crazy trip experiences or funny travel stories of the 'What was I thinking' kind.

Hayley: I have lots - I have travelled to Israel during the war and Egypt during the revolution, which was pretty dangerous but justified due to the CHEAP prices! I have missed my flight to Poland THREE times for various reasons and still haven't made it there... I hired a car with brakes that didn't work in Turkey. I have LOTS more but you will have to visit the blog for those... sign up to receive alerts when new posts are published!

Kunal: What is the one place you have visited that has been a life changing experience and changed the way you view the world?

My world views are not down where I have travelled, although of course you learn new things along the way. My views on life are a combination of the things I have seen and done, my experiences and my education. Of course traveling to a war zone or teaching English in another country teaches you a lot, but I wouldn't say any one place can change somebody's world views.

Kunal: Name the best and worst travel location you've been to and why?

Hayley: The Galapagos Islands were absolutely incredible. We went for our honeymoon earlier this year. It was like you could literally see evolution right before your eyes, truly spectacular.

I wasn't so impressed with Sharm El Sheikh (Egypt) - far too globalised. I have resort enclaves and I have even more when people tell me that they have been to 'Egypt' or any other resort in another country, when all they have seen is the airport, the view from the transfer bus and the hotel. There's nothing wrong with that if that's what you want, but say it like it is!

Kunal: Any words of wisdom for the young (and not so young ones) who may be thinking of embarking on a journey similar to yours?

Hayley: Just have fun, you only live once- get out there and see the world, look beyond the walls of your hotel and appreciate the wonders that this world has to offer!
