My Experience using Your Local Cousin while traveling to Edinburgh, Scotland


Edinburgh, Scotland

Emily Bichsel   Posted on 18 October, 2016

When I’m traveling, the last thing I want to be doing is sitting in my hotel room furiously Googling the best attractions and lunch spots. Sometimes I don’t have time to do thorough preparation before embarking on a great adventure. It can be enough just to get myself out the door! Planning on the fly is not a productive use of time. This September before my study abroad, I decided to take matters and put them into someone else’s hands: I enlisted the help of Your Local Cousin (YLC). Your Local Cousin is a service that allows you to contact a ‘Local Cousin’ for travel advice in your preferred destination. As I had several cities on the docket before commencing my studies in Paris, I didn’t have time to plan thoroughly for all of them. I decided to enlist the help of ‘Local Cousin’ Claire in Edinburgh. I opted for a phone conversation and interactive map with the local.

Claire’s profile on Your Local Cousin

Claire was immensely helpful in answering my questions and providing me trip advice tailored to my interests. She made many spot suggestions which were right in the heart of Edinburgh, while still being relatively overlooked by tourists. The customized Google map that Claire created made finding the good choices on the fly especially easy. We felt comfortable heading out for the day without having every detail planned to a T, knowing that we could consult the map for great suggestions in any area we might find ourselves. For example, we quickly consulted the map to find the Scottish Storytelling Centre. It doesn’t look like much from the outside and I suspect that the lack of a trendy interior probably scares some tourists away. The café isn’t well advertised from the street either. However Claire assured us that it was one of her favorite cafes to recharge, free from the throngs of tourists along the Royal Mile. Indeed she was right! We didn’t hear a single American (or other foreign) accent the whole time! All the customers appeared to be local Scottish people meeting for a casual lunch or coffee. What’s more, the food was delicious, came in generous portions, and was quite inexpensive. Earlier in the week, my sister and I left one of our lunches to chance and wandered in somewhere on the Royal Mile. We were sorely disappointed. Wandering is a great way to see a city, but when you have a very limited amount of time, having a general overview of an area and a few good suggestions from someone who knows the terrain can make a world of difference in the quality of your experience.

A great lunch at the Scottish Storytelling Centre

On our last day, we had a few hours to kill before catching a cab to the airport. My sister wanted to linger in a café, while I wasn’t done exploring, so I checked my map for one of Claire’s suggestions and realized that I was right next to the Surgeons’ Hall Museums. In our conversation Claire emphasized that an important part of the history of Edinburgh was its role as a hub for (often illegal) medical exploration and surgical experimentation. Though not for the feint of heart or weak stomached, the collection of surgical instruments, preserved body parts, and medical artifacts provided a fascinating introduction to the history of surgery (and definitely an appreciation of modern medicine…).

Surgeons’ Hall Museums- Edinburgh, Scotland

While we followed many of Claire’s suggestions (including Holyrood Palace, Edinburgh Castle, and Arthur’s Seat to name just a few) and enjoyed them all thoroughly, my other favorite experience she recommended was a visit to Portobello Beach. My sister and I love to walk, and I love the ocean, so a trip to the beach seemed like a natural choice. We asked Claire where to go and she pointed the way to Portobello. My sister and I spent an idyllic afternoon wandering around the Scottish seaside, making friends with local dogs and their owners, eating sandwiches, and indulging in the occasional windblown photo. We never would have stumbled upon this gem on our own.

A great day at Portobello Beach

As a student with a busy schedule and lots of homework during the week, I will definitely be enlisting the help of “local cousins” for my weekend trips. Because in Paris, I want to be enjoying Paris, not spending hours researching, reading Yelp reviews, and creating detailed lists of what I want to do to make the most of my time. I’ll let my ‘Local Cousin’ handle the details while I handle the fun!

Emily enjoying a great (and windy) day in Edinburgh
